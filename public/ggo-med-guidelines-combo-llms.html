GGO Med – Visual & Image Creation Guidelines

Authority, precision and inclusion for sensitive men’s health content.

Internal Brand Toolkit
Owner: GGO Med Clinic
Scope: All illustrations, infographics & social media visuals
Workflow A · Core Prompt

1. Medical Illustration Creation Prompt Anatomy / Condition

Purpose

Create a medically accurate yet patient-friendly illustration of [SPECIFIC ANATOMY/CONDITION] for use in GGO Med patient education.

Visual Specifications

  • Style: Clean, modern medical-textbook vector illustration.
  • Colour palette:
    • Teal #00EEB6 – primary anatomical elements.
    • Deep charcoal #4B4B4B – outlines, labelling, secondary details.
    • Soft blue #E3EEFD – background or gentle highlights.
    • Mint fresh #B5FFE7 – wellness/positive accents as needed.
  • Design characteristics: simplified but anatomically correct, rounded and soft edges, professional and approachable, with clear space for labels.
  • Background: clean white or very soft blue (#E3EEFD).

Output Requirements

  • High-resolution image suitable for print and web.
  • Vector or vector-style artwork preferred for scalability.
  • Strict adherence to GGO Med brand colours and tone.
Workflow A · Core Prompt

2. Infographic Creation Prompt Process / Journey

Purpose

Create an infographic explaining [SPECIFIC MEDICAL PROCESS / PATIENT JOURNEY].

Design Requirements

  • Layout: Clear flow (left-to-right or top-to-bottom).
  • Colour scheme:
    • Teal #00EEB6 – primary elements and connectors.
    • Soft blue #E3EEFD – background panels.
    • Charcoal #4B4B4B – text sections.
    • Mint #B5FFE7 – positive/success states.
  • Icons: simple, rounded, modern medical icons.
  • Typography: clear hierarchy for headings, key points and explanatory text.
  • Aesthetic: professional, calm and patient-centred, with generous white space and soft curves.

Brand Compliance

The infographic must be suitable for sensitive urology and men’s health topics, reflect GGO Med’s authoritative yet empathetic voice, and avoid graphic or sensational imagery.

Workflow A · Web Assets

3. Website Hero Image Prompt 16:9 Hero

Purpose

Create a contemporary website hero image for [SPECIFIC MEDICAL TOPIC/SERVICE].

Specifications

  • Format: 16:9 landscape hero.
  • Colour palette:
    • Soft blue #E3EEFD to white gradient background.
    • Teal #00EEB6 accents.
    • Charcoal #4B4B4B for any text areas.
  • Design elements: abstract medical forms or subtle anatomical references, soft organic shapes (no harsh geometry).
  • Mood: professional, reassuring, approachable and trustworthy.
  • Usability: generous white space for headline, subtext and call-to-action buttons.
Workflow A · Social Media

4. Social Media Graphic Prompt Platform-Specific

Purpose

Create a social media graphic for [PLATFORM] about [TOPIC].

Specifications

  • Formats: 1:1 square, 9:16 story, or 1.91:1 banner as required.
  • Background: soft blue (#E3EEFD) or white.
  • Primary elements: teal (#00EEB6).
  • Text areas: charcoal (#4B4B4B) on light backgrounds.
  • Accents: mint (#B5FFE7) for positive highlights.

Design Elements

  • Clean, legible type hierarchy.
  • Rounded containers and cards.
  • Modern medical icons when relevant.
  • Space for GGO Med logo/branding.
  • Professional but engaging layout, compliant with platform best practice for healthcare content.
Workflow A · Education

5. Patient Education Infographic Prompt A4 Print

Purpose

Create a patient-friendly educational infographic about [SPECIFIC CONDITION/TREATMENT].

Content Structure

  • Clear title area in charcoal (#4B4B4B).
  • Section dividers in teal (#00EEB6).
  • Information boxes on soft blue backgrounds (#E3EEFD).
  • Positive outcome indicators in mint (#B5FFE7).
  • Call-to-action areas and “next steps” in teal (#00EEB6).

Visual Approach

  • Clear information hierarchy and stepwise progression where applicable.
  • Reassuring, educational tone with simplified anatomical references.
  • Absolutely no graphic surgical imagery.

Layout Requirements

  • A4 proportion for printing and PDF use.
  • Generous margins and white space.
  • Logical reading flow (top-to-bottom and left-to-right).
  • Space reserved for GGO Med contact information and website link.
Workflow A · QA Step

6. Brand Compliance Review Prompt Checklist

Brand Colour Compliance

  • Confirm that only approved GGO Med colours are used: #00EEB6, #E3EEFD, #4B4B4B, #B5FFE7, #111111, #3208F5.
  • Ensure teal (#00EEB6) highlights key elements/focal points.
  • Soft blue (#E3EEFD) appears mainly as background or gentle highlight.

Visual Style Compliance

  • Professional yet approachable aesthetic maintained.
  • Rounded and soft corners used consistently.
  • Ample white space around key content.
  • No harsh, overly clinical or intimidating visuals.
  • Explicit suitability for sensitive men’s health topics.

Brand Consistency

  • Aligned with contemporary healthcare branding.
  • Visuals would sit comfortably alongside existing GGO Med assets.
  • Overall tone remains trustworthy, empathetic and evidence-based.

Where elements fail this audit, specify exact modifications (colour changes, spacing, edge rounding, icon style updates) required to restore full brand compliance.

Workflow A · Revision

7. Image Modification Prompt Refinement

Colour Adjustments

  • Change specified elements to precise GGO Med colours (e.g. “Change [element] to teal #00EEB6”).
  • Ensure primary focal areas use teal for emphasis.
  • Soften any overly harsh colour contrasts.

Style Refinements

  • Increase corner rounding where necessary.
  • Add white space around crowded elements.
  • Refine shapes and iconography to feel more approachable while remaining professional.

Layout Improvements

Specify concrete layout changes (e.g. re-aligning sections, adjusting hierarchy, balancing columns) whilst preserving all brand rules.

Workflow A · Journey Design

8. Patient Journey Infographic Prompt Horizontal Flow

Journey Stages

  1. Initial concern or symptoms.
  2. Decision to seek help.
  3. Booking a consultation.
  4. Assessment appointment.
  5. Treatment planning.
  6. Treatment implementation.
  7. Follow-up care.
  8. Positive outcomes and long-term support.

Visual Requirements

  • Horizontal flow with rounded containers for each stage.
  • Teal (#00EEB6) connecting arrows or lines.
  • Soft blue (#E3EEFD) stage backgrounds.
  • Mint (#B5FFE7) for outcome and reassurance elements.
  • Text in charcoal (#4B4B4B).
  • Clean white background with subtle supporting elements only.

Brand Approach

Emphasise expert support and confidence-building at each stage, reflecting GGO Med’s role as a calm, specialist partner throughout the patient journey.

Workflow B · Anatomy Focus

9. Workflow B – Anatomical Accuracy Brief Medical Detail

Medical Requirements

  • Anatomically accurate representation of [SPECIFIC CONDITION/ANATOMY].
  • Simplified for patient understanding (no unnecessary complexity).
  • Highlight required medical details (e.g. plaques, narrowed vessels, affected structures).
  • Suitable as a visual aid during consultant–patient discussion.

Brand Visual Requirements

  • Teal (#00EEB6) for primary structures.
  • Soft blue (#E3EEFD) or white background.
  • Charcoal (#4B4B4B) outlining and labelling.
  • Minimalist, modern medical style with rounded, soft edges.

Patient Perspective

  • Educational and reassuring rather than alarming.
  • Avoids graphic realism or anything that may provoke anxiety.
  • Explicitly suitable for sensitive topics within men’s health.
Workflow B · Educational Layer

10. Educational Enhancement for Illustrations Labelling

Enhancement Requirements

  • Include clear visual areas for labels and annotations.
  • Use visual hierarchy (size, weight, position) to guide the eye through the diagram.
  • Add subtle cues (e.g. soft highlighting, arrows) for key structures and problem areas.
  • Ensure the design actively supports the consultant’s verbal explanation.
Phase 6 · File Management

11. File Naming Convention System Consistency

Use the following naming pattern for all visual assets:

GGOMed_[ContentType]_[Topic]_[Date]_[Version]

Examples

  • GGOMed_Infographic_VaricoceleJourney_2024Jan15_v1.png
  • GGOMed_Illustration_ProstatectomyProcedure_2024Jan15_v2.png
  • GGOMed_SocialMedia_EDEducation_Instagram_2024Jan15_v1.png
  • GGOMed_WebBanner_FertilityConsultation_2024Jan15_v1.png
Phase 6 · Archiving

12. Quality Archive Metadata Prompt Documentation

For each final image, record the following:

Technical Specifications

  • Dimensions and resolution.
  • Primary colours used (with hex codes).
  • Best usage recommendations (web, print, social media etc.).

Content Description

  • Brief description of visual elements.
  • Intended use case (e.g. ED explainer, Peyronie’s landing page).
  • Key brand-compliance features (colour, style, sensitivity handling).

Usage Notes

  • Specific application suggestions (hero, inline illustration, downloadable PDF).
  • Accessibility considerations (contrast, text size, complexity).
  • Potential modifications for alternative formats.
Phase 7 · Consistency

13. Advanced Brand Consistency Prompt Style Matching

When creating new content types, ensure they are visually indistinguishable from established GGO Med materials in:

  • Colour palette application (same usage of teal, soft blue, charcoal, mint).
  • Illustration/design style (minimalist, rounded, calm, clinical-accurate).
  • Visual hierarchy (headings, subheadings, diagrams, key callouts).
  • White space and spacing patterns.
  • Professional yet approachable brand personality.
  • Accessibility and readability standards.
Phase 7 · Adaptation

14. Multi-Format Adaptation Prompt Responsive Design

Create optimised variations for the following formats:

  1. Website hero – 16:9 landscape.
  2. Social media square – 1:1.
  3. Social story – 9:16 vertical.
  4. Print materials – A4 proportion.

Adaptation Requirements

  • Maintain consistent brand colours and key visual elements.
  • Preserve core message and illustration focus in all versions.
  • Optimise text sizing and density for each format.
  • Ensure immediate brand recognition (logo, palette, style).
Phase 8–9 · Final QA

15. Final Brand Audit & Implementation Checklist Pre-Release

Visual Brand Compliance

  • Colour palette accuracy (exact hex codes used correctly).
  • Professional yet approachable aesthetic.
  • Appropriate use of white space.
  • Soft, rounded shapes and elements.
  • Contemporary, high-end healthcare feel.

Content Appropriateness

  • Suitable for sensitive men’s health topics.
  • Maintains dignity and respect for patients.
  • Medically accurate and evidence-aligned.
  • Educational and reassuring tone throughout.
  • Appropriate for the intended audience and context.

Technical Quality

  • High resolution with clear details.
  • Suitable for the intended use (web/print/social).
  • Accessibility considerations met (contrast, legibility).
  • Scalable to different sizes without quality loss.
  • Compatible across digital and print channels.

Brand Alignment

  • Reflects GGO Med’s expertise and empathy.
  • Consistent with existing brand assets and strategy.
  • Supports patient journey, experience and trust-building.

Implementation Checklist (Before Use)

  • Verify all colours match official hex codes.
  • Confirm aesthetic is professional but warm.
  • Check sensitivity and appropriateness for topic.
  • Ensure text and visual contrast meet accessibility standards.
  • Test scalability for all planned sizes.
  • Confirm alignment with brand voice and values.
  • Review technical quality (file format, compression, responsiveness).
  • Name the file following GGO Med convention.
  • Archive with full metadata and usage notes.
